Resources

Career advice

  • 80,000 Hours provides advice on how to have a greater social impact with your career. Their career guide is the best place to start if you want to learn how to have an impact. The ideas they teach will be important when choosing how to have an impact with technology.

Startup advice

  • Y Combinator’s class on how to start a startup and a summary of their advice.
  • Zero to One by Peter Thiel, who cofounded Paypal and Palantir, is a guide to building world-changing companies.
  • The Lean Startup describes how to test the assumptions behind a startup so that you don’t end up building something no-one wants.

Choosing what to work on

  • 80,000 Hours has a guide to selecting a cause to work on, which explains how to choose and which causes look promising.
  • Y Combinator’s request for startups lists areas that they’d like to see startups in that they think will make the world a better place.
  • What happened to the future? is a manifesto by Peter Thiel’s Founders Fund. It argues that venture capitalists should fund more transformational technologies and suggests some types of technology that they think are important.

The technology for good community

  • Tech for Good provides tech for good news and works on building the community.
  • impact.tech is a community of technologists who want to do good, with events in San Francisco.
  • London Tech For Good meetup.
  • Try searching meetup.com for ‘tech for good’ meetups in your area.

Online communities
